This document maps Common Criteria and protection profile clauses to a practical local-signing phone architecture, with concrete component examples and direct source references for each mapped clause.

This document is an unofficial informative report, dated February 20, 2026. It is not a specification, not a draft specification, and does not represent support or consensus from any standards organization.

This document is not a legal finding of qualified status. Legal qualified status requires the certification/listing path under eIDAS Article 29, Article 30, and Article 31 [[EIDAS910]].

Clause-level mapping here uses publicly downloadable SSCD PP documents (Part 2 and Part 5) plus CC Part 2/3 and CEM. EN 419 241 is included as migration target reference via CEN-CENELEC search [[CENSEARCH]].

Quick Cheat Sheet

Term Meaning / phone-side equivalent

SAR/EAL Mapping for “Ready to Apply”

PP Part 2 and Part 5 both target EAL4 augmented with AVA_VAN.5 [[PPSSCD2]] [[PPSSCD5]]. The outcome is “methodically designed, tested, and reviewed,” plus advanced vulnerability analysis [[CCPART3]].

Assurance block What must exist
ASE_* (ST evaluation)Complete ST with conformance claims, objectives, requirements, and TOE summary specification.
ADV_*Architecture, functional spec, implementation representation, and modular design evidence.
ALC_*Lifecycle, configuration management, delivery, and development security evidence.
ATE_*Coverage, design-based tests, and independent testing samples.
AVA_VAN.5Advanced methodical vulnerability analysis and penetration testing.

CEM work units and verdict rules are used to produce pass/fail evidence per assurance component [[CEMR5]].

Real-World Snapshot (As of 2026-02-20 Context Capture)

1. CC portal includes many QSCD/SSCD product records (for example SafeSign, ChipDoc, CombICAO, AKD eID, and DocuSign local-signing entries) [[CCPROD]].

3. In this captured dataset, no record appears as both “mobility category” and explicit “QSCD/SSCD” claim string at the same time.

Where No Direct Example Exists, What to Do

Gap Suggested fulfillment path
Trusted display proof for DTBS review before signingImplement dedicated trusted UI mode and cryptographically bind displayed DTBS hash to signature command.
Strong physical/emanation evidence at full-phone scopeNarrow TOE to secure element/enclave and compose with minimal trusted software boundary.
Admin/signatory role separation on consumer OSDefine explicit role model in ST and enforce role-restricted management APIs.
Opaque secure-channel internals in commodity stacksAdd explicit signed request/response wrapper between SCA and TOE channel endpoints.
Insufficient AVA_VAN.5 depth evidenceRun pre-cert penetration plan covering TEE escape, overlay attack, side-channel, and fault injection vectors.

Bottom Line

A high-end phone can be profiled toward local-QSCD behavior if its TOE boundary is strict, keys stay hardware-bound, trusted channels are explicit, and ST/SAR evidence is complete.
